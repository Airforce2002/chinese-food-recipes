Ingredient Spotlight

Soy Sauce: Liquid Umami

Soy sauce is the blood of Chinese cooking. This fermented condiment of soybeans and wheat carries thousands of years of culinary tradition in every drop. There are two essential types: light soy (for seasoning) and dark soy (for color and sweetness).

Quality matters. Look for naturally brewed soy sauce with minimal ingredients. The label should list soybeans, wheat, salt, and water — nothing more.

Ingredient Substitutes

When you can't find the authentic ingredient

Common Substitutions

Light Soy Sauce → Regular soy sauce
Shaoxing Wine → Dry sherry
Rice Vinegar → White wine vinegar
Sesame Oil → Peanut oil + few drops EO
Oyster Sauce → Soy + mushroom sauce

Harder to Substitute

Doubanjiang → Gochujang + soy (partial)
Sichuan Peppercorn → Black pepper (no numbing)
Chinkiang Vinegar → Balsamic (different flavor)
Five Spice → Star anise + cinnamon
Black Vinegar → Balsamic + soy
